Basics: a flat photo becomes a depth map

The core capability, plainly: give the ViT-Large model one ordinary photo and it estimates how far away every pixel is. Brighter / warmer is nearer; darker is further. That single map is the raw material for blur, parallax, and occlusion — but here we just look at it.

Look at the edges — fences, plant stems, the boundary where the subject meets the background. The Large encoder keeps those crisp where the small model tends to smear them. Same photo, sharper map.
