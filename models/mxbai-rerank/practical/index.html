Practical: rerank a keyword shortlist for RAG

Reranking is the precision stage of retrieval. A cheap first pass (here, a keyword filter) narrows a larger corpus to a shortlist, and the cross-encoder rereads each shortlisted passage against the query and reorders it — so the chunk you hand your LLM is the one that genuinely answers the question, not just the one with the most matching words. Both stages run locally.
