Wild: hard negatives that fool keyword search

A hard negative is a passage engineered to look relevant — it repeats the query's words, or is on the same topic — but doesn't actually answer it. These are what break keyword search and trip up embeddings. The cross-encoder reads query and passage together, so it can tell "about the topic" from "answers the question."
