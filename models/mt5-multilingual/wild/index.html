The pure objective: mask several spans at once

This is the exact task mT5 was pretrained on. During pretraining, ~15% of tokens are dropped in contiguous spans, each replaced by a single sentinel (<extra_id_0>, <extra_id_1>, …), and the model learns to emit those sentinels followed by the missing text. Mask a few spans yourself and read the raw sentinel-delimited target it produces — the format is the whole point. It works across languages from one shared vocabulary.

Read the raw output as pairs: <extra_id_0> then its fill, <extra_id_1> then its fill, and so on — usually ending with the next unused sentinel to mark "done". A small q8 model reconstructs short, high-frequency spans best; longer or rarer spans get rougher. That is the honest behaviour of the raw multilingual foundation.
