Wild: ask in one language, answer from another

Here's the party trick a single multilingual model unlocks: ask your question in English, but point it at a passage in French, German, Spanish or Italian. XLM-R maps every language into one shared representation, so the question and the foreign passage land in the same space — and the model can still pick out the answer span. The answer stays in the passage's language (it's an extracted span, not a translation).

Cross-lingual QA is genuinely hard, so expect lower confidence than the same-language case — the score readout is honest about that. When it works, it's doing something no keyword search or single-language model can: bridging the question and the answer across a language boundary, entirely on-device.
