Read the TL;DR aloud: article → summary → speech

Two browser models in one pipeline. BART-large-CNN boils an article down to a short summary; then Kokoro speaks that summary aloud in a natural voice. Text in, spoken recap out — a hands-free briefing, and nothing ever leaves the tab.

Each model initialises on its own below (cached copies auto-start, no button).

Why this is the interesting part

Summarization and speech are each useful alone. Chained, they're a template for a whole class of tool: a commute briefing that reads you the morning's news, an accessibility reader that condenses and voices long pages, a study aid that turns dense chapters into spoken recaps. Swap in speech-to-text at the front and you have voice-in, voice-out — all local, no server, no API bill.
