Multi-model: domain vs general sentiment, head to head

Two text classifiers, one sentence. FinBERT was tuned on financial text and has a neutral class; DistilBERT SST-2 learned tone from movie reviews and only knows positive/negative. Run a finance sentence through both and the domain gap shows up: the general model forces market-neutral notices into positive or negative, and can misread jargon that FinBERT reads correctly. That gap is the whole reason a domain model exists.

Both models run entirely on-device, in separate Web Workers, so the comparison is a genuine two-model pipeline with no server. The takeaway: pick the model whose training domain matches your text. A general sentiment model is fine for reviews; for market language, the finance-tuned one — with its neutral class — is the honest choice. Still tone, still not advice.
