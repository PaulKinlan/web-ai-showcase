Wild: where a tone model breaks

Sentiment is tone, not understanding. FinBERT is strong on plain financial statements — but sarcasm, heavy hedging, double negatives, and headlines that need world knowledge can all fool it. Load a trap, read FinBERT's verdict, and notice how confident it can be while missing the point. Poke at it with your own edge cases too.

The lesson isn't that FinBERT is bad — it's that a classifier maps surface language to a label without reasoning about it. High confidence on a sarcastic line is a reminder to keep a human in the loop and to treat sentiment as one weak signal among many, never a decision.
