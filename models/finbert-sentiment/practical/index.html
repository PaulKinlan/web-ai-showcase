Practical: triage a headline feed by tone

A real desk chore: a stream of headlines arrives and you want to see, at a glance, what's negative and needs attention. Paste one headline per line and FinBERT scores the batch in a single pass, sorts it most-negative first, and tallies the tone. Signal, not advice — a triage aid, not a trading system.

positive – neutral – negative – net tone – backend – latency –

Sorted most-negative first — the tone that usually needs a human's eyes soonest.

One forward pass scores the whole batch, so a feed of dozens of headlines classifies in well under a second on the WASM backend — all in the tab, no upload. Because it reports tone and not magnitude, a small "beat" and a blow-out quarter can both read simply positive; use it to sort and surface, then let a person judge.
