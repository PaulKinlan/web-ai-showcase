Wild: reason through a riddle or plan

The reasoning trace is the entertainment. Give the model a lateral-thinking riddle, a classic river-crossing, or a "plan this for me" task, and watch it explore, backtrack, and converge — out loud — before it answers. Expand the reasoning panel and follow along. All on your GPU, nothing uploaded.

Why the reasoning is worth watching

On a riddle, a non-reasoning model tends to pattern-match to a memorised answer — sometimes the wrong one. A reasoning model can catch itself: it lays out constraints, tests a move, notices it fails, and tries another. That's why the trace is interesting for hard or trick questions — you see where it nearly goes wrong and how it recovers (or, sometimes, where it convinces itself of something false — equally instructive). The answer is the model's final commitment; the reasoning is how it got there.
