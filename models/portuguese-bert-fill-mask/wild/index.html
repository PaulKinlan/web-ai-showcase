Wild: Brazilian vs European Portuguese & idioms

BERTimbau read only Brazilian Portuguese. So when a word has a Brazilian form and a European-Portuguese twin, which does it reach for? Score both at the same [MASK] and watch the model overwhelmingly back the Brazilian variant. Then feed it famous Portuguese sayings and see whether it absorbed the phrase, not just the grammar.

Brazilian vs European — which word does it prefer?

Each pair is the same everyday thing named two ways: the Brazilian word and the European-Portuguese one. The model scores each at the [MASK]; because it only ever read Brazilian text, the Brazilian form usually wins by a landslide.

These are real softmax probabilities at the masked position. A near-zero for the European word doesn't mean it's wrong Portuguese — it means the Brazilian corpus this model read almost never used it.

Idiom completion — does it know the saying?

Complete a Portuguese saying from a single [MASK]. Idioms are non-compositional — the missing word doesn't follow from grammar alone — so a hit means the model actually met the phrase in training. It won't nail every one; that's the point of probing.
