Voice loop: speak → transcribe → edit → speak back

Two browser models wired into one pipeline. Whisper turns your voice into text; you tweak the words; then Kokoro speaks them back in a voice you choose. Speech in, speech out — and nothing ever leaves the device.

This loop chains two on-device models — each initialises on its own below (cached copies auto-start, no button).

Why this is the interesting part

On their own, each model is useful. Chained, they're a template for a whole class of app: a private voice assistant, a pronunciation coach, a "re-voice this in a calmer tone" tool, a hands-free note-taker that reads your notes back. Drop an on-device LLM between steps 2 and 3 and you have a full voice agent — with no server, no API bill, and no audio ever leaving the tab.
