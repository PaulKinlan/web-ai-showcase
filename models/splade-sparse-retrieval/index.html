Learned sparse retrieval (SPLADE)

The built embedding models turn text into a dense vector of opaque numbers. SPLADE does something different: it produces a sparse vector — a weight per word in the vocabulary, mostly zero — and it expands the text with related terms it never contained ("pasta" also lights up "noodles", "italy", "food"). That's how neural lexical search matches meaning while staying an interpretable, invertible index. Encode a sentence, then match a second one. Runs on your device.

Why raw ONNX Runtime Web?

transformers.js has no SPLADE task, so we run the masked-LM ONNX directly with onnxruntime-web and tokenize with a transformers.js tokenizer. SPLADE reads the model's word-prediction logits at every position and keeps, for each vocabulary word, its strongest log(1 + relu) score — a sparse vector where a handful of words carry weight, including words the text implies but never wrote. Two texts are relevant when their sparse vectors overlap. Everything runs on your device.
