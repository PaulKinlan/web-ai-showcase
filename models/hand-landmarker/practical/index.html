Practical: a gesture-driven UI

Landmarks aren't the point — what you do with them is. This turns the 21 points into a simple controller: it counts how many fingers are raised and names the gesture (fist, peace, point, thumbs up, rock). Try it on a sample hand, or drive it live with your webcam.

Where this goes

Map each gesture to an action and you have a touchless remote: a fist to pause, an open palm to stop, a point to select, a thumbs up to confirm. Because it's all on-device, it works offline and nothing is streamed anywhere — practical for kiosks, accessibility, cooking/workshop hands-busy scenarios, and presentations.
