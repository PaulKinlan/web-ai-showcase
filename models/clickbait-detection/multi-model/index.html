Clickbait + sentiment: bait vs plain bad news

Clickbait tells you how a headline is trying to grab you; sentiment tells you the emotional direction. Chain them: score the bait level with DistilBERT headline-classifier and the polarity with DistilBERT SST-2, then combine to tell apart loud emotional bait from calm, negative-but-straight reporting — the kind of headline that's grim but honest. Both models run locally; nothing leaves the tab.

Why this is the interesting part

Neither model can do this alone. Sentiment calls both "grim honest report" and "loud emotional bait" negative; clickbait flags the loud one but says nothing about direction. Cross the two axes — bait level × polarity — and you get a useful 2×2: calm straight news, grim-but-honest reporting, upbeat bait, and emotional bait. That's a tiny piece of glue turning two 65-MB classifiers into a headline triage tool. It's a toy rule (real systems would weight by confidence and defer to a human near the boundary), but it shows the compositional move: small local models, chained, read a headline more richly than either alone.
