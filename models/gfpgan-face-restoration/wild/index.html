Find where restoration invents identity

GFPGAN reconstructs from a generative face prior — so the more degraded the input, the more of the face is invented rather than recovered. Degrade harder and watch the reconstruction drift into a plausible but different person. A vivid lesson in what a face-GAN can and can't be trusted to do.

What you're seeing

At mild degradation GFPGAN recovers the real features. Crank it up and the input carries almost no identity — so the model confidently fills in a plausible face from its prior, and the result can look sharp yet be subtly (or not so subtly) a different person. That's the honest limit of generative face restoration: never treat a heavily-restored face as proof of who was there.
