Restore the face, then upscale the photo

This is the classic real-world restoration pipeline: GFPGAN reconstructs the face from a learned prior, then Real-ESRGAN enlarges the whole restored crop 4× and rebuilds general detail. Two specialist models, chained, running entirely on your device — the exact division of labour the original GFPGAN pipeline uses.

restore –upscale –size –backend –

Both models run on-device; the photo and every restored pixel never leave the tab.

Order matters

Restore the face before upscaling: GFPGAN reconstructs facial structure at 512×512, then Real-ESRGAN enlarges that clean result 4× and sharpens general texture. Doing it the other way would upscale the degraded face and bake in its artefacts. Each model does the job it's best at — the face specialist first, the general upscaler second.
