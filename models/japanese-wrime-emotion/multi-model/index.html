Multi-model: fine-grained emotion vs coarse polarity

Two models, one Japanese sentence. On the left, this page's Japanese-native model scores Plutchik's eight emotions. On the right, the showcase's multilingual XLM-R scores the same text as negative / neutral / positive. Watching them side by side shows what an eight-emotion, language-specialist model buys you over three-way polarity — and surfaces the interesting cases where they disagree (a surprised or fearful message the polarity model calls "neutral"). Both run entirely on your device.

Load both models (the emotion model ~156 MB; the multilingual sentiment model ~279 MB, on demand), then score any Japanese text through both at once.
