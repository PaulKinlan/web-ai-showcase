Wild: keyword-stuffed hard negatives

The case that breaks keyword search: decoys packed with the query's exact words but answering the wrong question, next to the real answer that barely repeats the query at all. A word-overlap score ranks the keyword-stuffed decoys first; the cross-encoder reads for meaning and pulls the genuine answer to the top. The contrast below ranks by the cross-encoder on the left and by shared-word overlap on the right — where they disagree is exactly where a reranker earns its keep.

Cross-encoder rank vs. word-overlap rank

The lexical column rewards passages that echo the query's words, so the keyword-stuffed decoys float to the top there — exactly where a naive keyword search would rank them. The cross-encoder reads what each passage actually says and promotes the one that answers the question, even when it shares almost no words with the query.
