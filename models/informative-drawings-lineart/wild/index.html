Which scenes ink up most?

Every scene draws differently. Trace one after another and this page keeps a running leaderboard of "ink coverage" — the fraction of the frame the model turned into line — so you can see what the model finds worth drawing: a clean portrait inks less, a dense cityscape or texture inks more.

What it tells you

Ink coverage is a rough measure of how much structure the model decided the scene needed. It's not just "busyness" — the model draws lines that convey shape, so a textured wall (lots of contrast, little structure) can ink less than a face (little contrast, lots of meaningful contour). Comparing scenes is a quick window into what "informative" means to this model.
