One query, one passage, one number

Everything Arctic does starts here: embed two pieces of text, take the cosine of their vectors, and you have a similarity score. The twist is the asymmetry — the query gets a retrieval prefix, the passage doesn't. Type a query and a passage, and see the score both ways. (On a single pair the prefix often lowers the absolute cosine — its real payoff is corpus-scale ranking, not one number — so treat Δ as "how much the prefix moved things", not "better or worse".)
