Negation, sarcasm, dialect & compounds

Textbook German is easy. Real German is stacked negation (nicht schlecht, gar nicht so übel), irony, regional dialect and monstrous compound nouns. Pick a challenge below to see how a German-native model handles it, with the word-level attribution so you can watch what it latched onto. Sarcasm is the honest weak spot: the surface words say one thing, the meaning is the opposite.

Pick a challenge, or type your own

What to look for

  • Negation stacking: a German-trained model usually reads "nicht schlecht" / "gar nicht so übel" as mildly positive — a naive keyword model flips on the word "schlecht/übel".
  • Sarcasm: hardest of all. "Toll, schon wieder kaputt" is positive on the surface, negative in meaning — expect the model to be fooled sometimes. That's honest, not a bug you can hide.
  • Compounds: long compound nouns (Kundenservicekatastrophe) are single tokens' worth of meaning — watch how the encoder weights them.
