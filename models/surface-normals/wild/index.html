Wild · cinematic relighting

Three-point colour light rig

Photographers light a face with three lamps — a bright key, a soft fill, and a rim to separate subject from background. With a per-pixel normal field you can do the same to a flat photo, in colour, on-device. Pick a look, or tune each light. All shading is N·L summed over the three lights.

~444 MB model, CPU path: the one-time estimate takes ~60–90 s in WebAssembly. Then relighting all three lights is real-time.

Studio lighting from a single photo

Every one of these lights is a direction and a colour; each pixel adds up max(0, N·L) × colour across the three. Because the geometry (the normals) is real and per-pixel, the highlights land where a real lamp would put them — the jaw catches the key, the far cheek picks up the fill, the hair edge glows under the rim. No 3D model, no relight pass on a server; just a normal map and a dot product.
