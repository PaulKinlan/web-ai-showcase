Practical: separate the thinking from the answer

A reasoning model is most useful when you can use the reasoning and the answer independently — show the working to a learner, but hand a clean answer to the rest of your app. Baguettotron reasons inside a <think> block; this page streams the thought process into one panel and the final answer into another, splitting them at the </think> boundary. All on-device.

Why split them?

The reasoning trace is great for transparency and teaching — you can see how the model got there — but you usually want to store, display, or act on just the answer. Splitting at </think> gives you both: a visible chain of thought and a clean payload. If the model runs out of tokens before closing the think block, this page honestly shows everything as reasoning-in-progress.
