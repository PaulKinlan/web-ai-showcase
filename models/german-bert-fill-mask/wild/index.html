Wild: German idioms & compound words

A model that only ever read German should have absorbed German idioms — and it should know how German famously glues words into long compounds. Complete a saying from a single [MASK], then feed it a monster compound noun and watch its WordPiece tokenizer chop it into German subwords.

Idioms are non-compositional — the missing word doesn't follow from grammar alone. If the model nails "Bahnhof" or "Hund", it learned the phrase, not the rule.

Compound splitter — how German glues words

Type a long German compound noun (or pick one). The model's German WordPiece tokenizer breaks it into subword pieces — dashed chips are ## continuations. A German-only vocabulary can express these in a few German morphemes instead of spelling them out.
