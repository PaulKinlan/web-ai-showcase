Wild: drug–event radar

Adverse-event surveillance starts with a simple question: did a medication and an adverse sign, symptom or condition show up together? Point the model at a dense block of clinical text and it extracts every drug and every finding, then flags the pairs that co-occur closely — a first-pass pharmacovigilance signal, entirely on-device. It's the kind of scan you could run over a corpus of notes without any of them leaving the machine.

backend – latency – drugs – events – signals –

Co-occurrence is not causation — this flags proximity, not a proven adverse reaction, and is a research/education demo, not a pharmacovigilance system. "Window" is the maximum character distance between a drug span and a finding span for them to be paired. Inference runs in a Web Worker; the pairing maths is a light main-thread pass.
