Basics: one blank, ranked guesses

The simplest thing masked language modelling does: hide one word and rank what belongs there. Pick an example or write your own with a single [MASK], and watch the ranked list — with the probability and the raw logit for each guess — update as you type.

The bar is the softmax probability; the logit is the raw score the network produced before normalising. On a strongly-constrained slot one guess towers over the rest; on an open slot the probability spreads thin — a direct read on how sure the model is.
