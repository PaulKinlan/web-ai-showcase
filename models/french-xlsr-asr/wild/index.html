What happens when you feed it the wrong language?

This model's encoder was pre-trained on 53 languages, but its output head only speaks French. Feed it English and it does the only thing it can: map the sounds it hears to the nearest French letters — a phonetic transliteration. It's a striking demonstration that an ASR model has no idea what language you meant; it only knows its own alphabet. Compare the two clips.

What's happening

On the French clip you get real words. On the English clip you get plausible-sounding French phonetics that are nonsense as French — the acoustic model is honestly reporting "these are the French phonemes closest to what I heard". This is why a language-specialised recogniser is a genuinely different model from the English wav2vec2: the fine-tuning bakes the target language's alphabet and phonotactics into the output, even though the XLSR-53 backbone underneath is shared.
