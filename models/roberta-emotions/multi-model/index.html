use case · multi-model · M2M100 + RoBERTa GoEmotions

Emotion detection in any language

This model is trained on English. So to read the emotion of a message in French, Japanese, or Arabic, we chain two browser models: M2M100 translates the text into English, then RoBERTa GoEmotions scores its 28 emotions. Paste something in any of 100 languages and get its emotional profile — nothing ever leaves the device.

Each model initialises on its own below (cached copies auto-start, no button).

Why this is the interesting part

Translation and emotion detection are each useful alone. Chained, they turn an English-only classifier into a multilingual one — read how a customer in any of 100 languages feels, without translating your model, without a server, and without a word leaving the tab. Swap M2M100 for Whisper in front and you can read the emotion of spoken foreign audio too; the building blocks compose.
