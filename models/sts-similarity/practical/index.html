Practical: deduplicate with a real threshold

A graded score lets you set a cutoff. Paste a list of sentences — support questions, survey answers, bug titles — pick a similarity threshold, and the model scores every pair and flags the ones that are near-duplicates. This is the honest version of "are these the same?": not a guess, a number you can tune.

All pairs, most similar first — highlighted rows are at or above your threshold.

backend –latency –pairs –duplicates –

Note how "How do I reset my password?", "What's the way to change my password?" and "I forgot my password, how do I recover it?" all cluster high, while "How long does shipping take?" and "When will my order arrive?" form a separate near-duplicate pair — different words, same intent. That's the cross-encoder reading meaning, not matching keywords.
