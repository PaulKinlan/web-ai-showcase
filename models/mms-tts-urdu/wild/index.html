Wild: the stochastic prosody lab

Most small TTS models are deterministic — same text, byte-identical audio, forever. VITS is not. Its stochastic duration predictor samples how long each sound lasts, so every run has its own timing. Generate the same Urdu sentence a few times below and compare: same words, different rhythm, different length. Nothing here is randomised by us — the variation is the model's own.

Each card is an independent synthesis of the exact same Urdu string. The bar shows that run's duration relative to the longest so far. A wider spread means the duration predictor is sampling more freely for this text; a hard, punchy sentence varies less than a flowing one.
