Wild: the edges where it gets it wrong

A toxicity classifier is a pattern-matcher, not a mind-reader. It has no model of intent, context, or who is speaking — so sarcasm, reclaimed slurs, quoted abuse, and neutral mentions of hard topics all trip it up. Click each case and watch the scores: the point is to see the calibration limits, so you never wire one of these straight to an automated ban.
