Wild: ambiguous & plated dishes

A food classifier lives or dies on the crop. Zoom into a single ingredient, rotate the plate, or cover half the dish, then re-identify and watch the top guess — and its confidence — wobble between visual neighbours (pizza ↔ lasagna, cupcake ↔ red-velvet cake). It's the fastest way to feel where a 101-dish model gets confused.

Tip: identify once with everything at zero to set a baseline, then push one slider at a time. A big drop in the top probability (or a jump in entropy toward 1) means the model has lost the plot — and the runners-up show which dishes it thinks look alike.
