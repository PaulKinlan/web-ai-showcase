Wild: hard negatives that fool word-matching

A hard negative is a passage stuffed with the query's exact words that nonetheless answers the wrong question — the kind of thing keyword search ranks first. Because a cross-encoder reads the query and passage together, even this tiny one can catch the mismatch. The contrast below ranks by the cross-encoder on the left and by shared-word overlap on the right, so you can watch the trap passages fall.
