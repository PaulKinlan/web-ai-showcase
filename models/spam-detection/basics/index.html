Basics: spam or ham, live

The simplest thing this model does: read a message and decide whether it looks like spam or a real message, with a confidence for each. Type in the box and the verdict updates a moment after you pause. Swap "free prize" for "lunch tomorrow" and watch the meter swing.

The model was trained on spammy promotional and scam text, so it is confident on classic junk (prizes, "act now", suspicious links) and on plain conversational messages — and wobblier on short, ambiguous, or off-domain text. Check the confidence, not just the label.
