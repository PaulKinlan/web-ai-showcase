Sketch recognition (QuickDraw)

The built image classifiers read photos. This one reads doodles. Draw something in the box and a MobileViT — trained on Google's QuickDraw — guesses what it is, live, from 345 categories. It's the "Quick, Draw!" game running entirely on your device: no photo, no upload, just your sketch. Try a cat, a house, a star, a ladder…

Why raw ONNX Runtime Web?

QuickDraw sketches are grayscale — one channel — so the model's first convolution expects a single-channel image, which the transformers.js image pipeline (built for 3-channel photos) can't feed. We run the ONNX directly: tight-crop your drawing to its ink, centre it, shrink it to 28×28, and pass white-on-black pixels straight to the model. It classifies as you draw, and every stroke stays on your device.
