use case · practical

Practical: cross-language semantic search

A real knowledge base keeps each article in whatever language it was written in. Embed all of them into one 768-d space, embed the query (with the model's "query: " retrieval prefix), and rank by cosine. A question in English surfaces the right answer even if it's written in Spanish, Japanese or German — no per-language index, no translation up front.

Retrieve across languages

A mixed-language knowledge base of long passages. Ask in any language; arctic-embed ranks every passage by meaning regardless of language.

Green badge = a hit in a different language from your query.

backend –latency –

This is the retrieval half of multilingual RAG: one shared-space embedder over the whole corpus. Because arctic-embed is Matryoshka-trained, in production you could store 256-d truncated vectors to shrink the index — see the Wild demo.
