Wild: Matryoshka — how small can the vectors go?

Arctic-embed-m-v2.0 is trained with Matryoshka Representation Learning: the 768-d vector packs the most important information into its first dimensions, so you can chop it to 512, 256, 128 or even 64 dims (and re-normalize) and it still works. Smaller vectors mean a smaller index and faster search. Here's the stress test — embed once at full width, then slide the dimension down and watch the same cross-lingual search hold its ranking, until it finally starts to break.

The index-size figure is relative to the full 768-d vector. In practice you'd pick the smallest width that keeps your retrieval quality acceptable — often 256 dims, a 3× smaller index, decided without re-embedding anything. That's the whole point of Matryoshka: one model, many vector sizes.
