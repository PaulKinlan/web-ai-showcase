Wild: tilt a flat photo in 3D

A single photo has no depth — until this model gives it one. With a depth value per pixel we can fake a viewpoint change: move your pointer over the image and near things slide further than far things, the way they would if you leaned around them. That's the "3D photo" effect, from one ordinary picture.

backend –depth latency –warp –

Each frame, every output pixel samples the photo shifted by (depth − ½) × tilt — a backward warp, so near pixels (high depth) move most. It runs on the main thread only while you move; on prefers-reduced-motion it holds a gentle static offset instead of animating.

Reduced-motion is on: showing a fixed tilt rather than following your pointer.
