Find the whiteout breaking point

Crank the haze from a light mist toward a total whiteout and watch the model fight to recover the scene. Somewhere the contrast it can restore stops keeping up with the haze you add — that's the physical limit of pulling a signal back out of near-nothing.

Why it breaks

Dehazing inverts I = J·t + A·(1−t): as transmission t → 0, the clear scene J is multiplied by almost zero, so almost none of it reaches the sensor. No network can divide by zero — past a certain haze density the original signal is simply gone, and what comes back is the model's best guess, not recovered detail. The "recovery" ratio (out-contrast ÷ in-contrast) climbs, then stalls as you push past what's physically invertible.
