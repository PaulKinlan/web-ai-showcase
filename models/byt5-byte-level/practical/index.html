Nothing is ever out-of-vocabulary

Every product eventually meets input its tokenizer never trained on: a new emoji, a right-to-left script, pasted code, a name in an unusual alphabet, or plain keyboard-mash. A sub-word vocabulary fragments or emits [UNK]. ByT5 can't — its "vocabulary" is the 256 byte values, so any byte sequence is representable. Paste real user text and watch it encode, always.
