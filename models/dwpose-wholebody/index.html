Whole-body pose estimation (DWPose)

The built body pose demo finds 17 skeleton joints. Real motion capture needs more — hands and face carry most of the expression. DWPose (a distilled RTMPose) returns 133 keypoints in one pass: 17 body + 6 feet + 68 face landmarks + 21 points on each hand. Below it runs on real runner crops; toggle the face and hands to see what "whole-body" adds. Runs entirely on your device.

Why raw ONNX Runtime Web?

Transformers.js has no RTMPose/DWPose class, so we run the ONNX graph directly with onnxruntime-web: expand a person box to the model's aspect, resize to 288×384, ImageNet-normalize, and decode the two SimCC heads (a 1-D coordinate classification per axis) into 133 keypoints. It's top-down — it estimates one already-cropped person — and it all happens on your device.
