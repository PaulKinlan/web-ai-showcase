Wild: the ambiguity meter

Real stories cross desks — a stadium financing bill is both Sports and Politics; a biotech IPO is both Health and Business. Because this head is multi-label, it can score several desks high at once. Craft a cross-topic headline and watch a live ambiguity meter — the runner-up desk's score relative to the top one — swing from confident to genuinely torn.

The meter is the runner-up desk's score divided by the top desk's (0 = one clearly dominant desk, →100% = a close second). Because the head is multi-label, a genuine cross-topic headline pushes two or more bars high and drives the meter right, while a single-topic headline pins it left. This is the model's own scoring — not a heuristic layered on top.
