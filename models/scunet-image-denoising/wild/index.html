Find the noise-blizzard breaking point

Crank the noise from a light grain toward a total blizzard and watch SCUNet fight to recover the image. Past a point, noise and real texture become indistinguishable — and the denoiser has to smooth away detail to kill the grain. That trade-off is the edge of what denoising can do.

Why it breaks

A denoiser has to decide, per pixel, whether a wiggle is noise or texture. At low noise that's easy; at extreme noise, fine detail and grain have the same statistics, so removing all the noise means removing real detail too. Watch the cleaned side get progressively softer as you push σ up — that's not a bug, it's the fundamental noise/detail trade-off every denoiser faces.
