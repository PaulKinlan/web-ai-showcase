Wild — segment an idea, not a noun

Because CLIPSeg rides on CLIP's language understanding, the prompt doesn't have to be a concrete object. Ask for the warmest area, the safest place to walk, or where to look first and it will still paint a heatmap — its best guess at where that idea lives in the pixels. Some hits are uncanny; some are nonsense. That's the fun.

Why this works at all

CLIPSeg conditions a segmentation decoder on a CLIP text embedding. CLIP learned those embeddings from hundreds of millions of image–caption pairs, so "cozy", "dangerous", or "warm" already point somewhere in its visual space. The decoder just asks "which pixels look most like that?" Treat the result as vibes, not ground truth — but it's a striking demo of language steering pixels, with no labels in sight.
