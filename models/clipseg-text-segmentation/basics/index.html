Basics — one phrase, one mask

The whole idea in its simplest form: each phrase you type becomes its own per-pixel mask. Enter a few and see them side by side — the model scores the same photo separately against each phrase.

What you're seeing

Each card is one forward-pass score map, thresholded into a mask. Notice that phrases the photo doesn't contain come back nearly empty — CLIPSeg isn't forced to pick a winner per pixel the way a fixed-class segmenter is; every phrase is judged on its own. Try a phrase that isn't there (a dog on the cats photo) and watch the mask stay dark.
