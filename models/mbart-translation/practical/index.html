Practical: any-to-any pairs, with a round-trip fidelity check

mBART is many-to-many, so you can go straight between any two of its 50 languages. Enter lines of text, pick a target, and translate. The round-trip check translates each result back to the source so you can eyeball which ones stayed intact and which drifted — a fast confidence signal for languages you may not read, all on-device.

lines –target – backend –time –

A round-trip match is a good sign; a drift means the meaning may have shifted and a human should check. It's a heuristic, not proof — but for a language you can't read, it's a genuinely useful signal.
