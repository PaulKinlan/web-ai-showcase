Retrieve cross-lingually with BGE-M3, then translate the winner

The front half of production multilingual RAG, made of two on-device models. First BGE-M3 finds the passage that best answers your query whatever language it's in, embedding whole passages in its 1024-d space. Then, if the winner isn't in your language, M2M100 translates it so you can read it. Language-agnostic retrieval first, expensive translation only where it's needed.

Why compose them?

Translating the whole corpus up front is wasteful — most documents will never match a given query. BGE-M3 embeds cheaply and language-agnostically, so you retrieve first across the entire multilingual corpus, then spend the heavier translation pass only on the passage a user actually lands on. It's the exact shape of production multilingual search — a strong shared-space retriever followed by a precise, heavier step — here both running privately on-device. (Two big models: ~570 MB + ~500 MB, cached after first load.)
