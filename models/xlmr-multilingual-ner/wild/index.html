Wild: tag entities in languages it never trained on

The model was fine-tuned for NER on ten languages — but XLM-RoBERTa's backbone was pretrained on a hundred. That shared representation is strong enough that it tags people, orgs and places in languages it saw no labelled entities for: Turkish, Swedish, Japanese, Indonesian, Polish. This "zero-shot cross-lingual transfer" is the whole reason XLM-R is the go-to multilingual encoder. Try the samples — or paste text in a language of your own.

Transfer isn't magic: accuracy is lower than for the trained languages, and it leans on names that look familiar across scripts. But for the long tail of languages with no dedicated NER model, "pretty good, on-device, free" is often exactly what you need.
