Wild: thinking on vs off, side by side

SmolLM3 is one model with two modes. Here we run the same prompt both ways at once: left with /no_think (fast, direct), right with /think (reasons in a <think> trace first). Watch the accuracy and latency difference for yourself — the clearest way to see what a reasoning budget actually buys. All on your GPU.

What the trace buys

On these puzzles the direct answer often gives the intuitive-but-wrong response, while the reasoning pass catches the trap — at the cost of extra tokens and latency. That trade-off is the whole point of a hybrid model: you spend the reasoning budget only on the prompts that need it. The exact same weights produce both columns; only the enable_thinking switch differs.
