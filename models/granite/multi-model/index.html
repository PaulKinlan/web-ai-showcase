On-device RAG: MiniLM retrieves, Granite answers

Two models, one pipeline, zero server — the enterprise "chat with your handbook" pattern. MiniLM turns a policy document into vectors and finds the passages closest to the question; Granite 3.0 2B — IBM's RAG-tuned model — then answers only from those passages and cites them. Because it all runs on-device, the handbook and the questions never leave the browser.

How the pipeline works

  1. Embed — MiniLM (feature-extraction, mean-pooled + normalised) maps each passage and the question to a 384-dim vector.
  2. Retrieve — cosine similarity ranks the passages; the top 3 are kept.
  3. Generate — the retrieved passages become Granite's context with an instruction to answer only from them and say "I don't know" otherwise — the guard against hallucination, and exactly the governance an enterprise needs.

Change a passage and the retrieval changes; ask something not in the handbook and a good RAG answer declines. Everything — both models, the vectors, the similarity — runs on your device.
