Multi-model: image vectors beside text vectors

Embeddings are the same idea in every modality — turn a thing into a vector, and similarity becomes a distance. Here two models make that concrete, both running locally. DINOv2 embeds a set of images; MiniLM embeds their captions. Two separate spaces, two similarity matrices — and the same groupings emerge in pixels and in words.

The two matrices are computed in totally separate embedding spaces — DINOv2 never sees the text, MiniLM never sees the pixels — yet the block structure (the two cats together, the two cars together) lines up. That shared geometry is what makes cross-modal systems like CLIP possible.
