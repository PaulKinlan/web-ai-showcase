Wild: one question in six languages, one ranking

A multilingual cross-encoder should judge relevance by meaning, not by the language the words are in. Here's the stress test: a fixed answer bank written in several languages, and the same question asked in six. Click through the languages — the ranking (and usually the winner) barely moves. That stability is the whole point of training one model on mMARCO across 14 languages instead of a separate model per locale.

Ask “How do I lower my monthly electricity bill?” in:

question lang –backend –latency –

The five energy-saving tips (in five different languages) should stay above the stock-market and café lines no matter which language you ask in; the two off-topic lines stay at the bottom. Meaning wins, not language.
