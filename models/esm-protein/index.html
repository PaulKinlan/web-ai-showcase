Protein language model (ESM-2)

The built fill-mask demos predict the missing word in a sentence. ESM-2 does the same for a different language entirely — proteins. Trained on millions of sequences, it predicts which amino acid belongs at a position, having learned the grammar of real proteins. It's the family behind structure prediction (ESMFold) and variant-effect scoring. Click a residue to see what it predicts, or scan a whole protein to map which positions are conserved. Runs entirely on your device.

A different language

ESM-2 is a masked language model like BERT, but its vocabulary is the 20 amino acids instead of words. By learning to fill in masked residues across millions of natural proteins, it internalises which substitutions proteins tolerate — so a masked conserved position (structurally or functionally critical) is predicted confidently, while a flexible one is uncertain. That signal is what larger ESM models turn into structure and mutation-effect predictions. Everything runs locally.
