Multi-model: fill the mask, then translate

A two-stage on-device pipeline. First Chinese BERT fills your [MASK] with the most likely character. Then a second model — a MarianMT Chinese→English translator — reads the completed sentence and renders it in English, so you can see exactly what the fill means. Two jobs, chained locally, nothing leaving the tab.

Models

This demo loads TWO models: the ~103 MB Chinese fill-mask model (the one the other demos use, cached if you've run them) and a ~75 MB translator.

1 · Fill-mask model

2 · Translation model (zh→en)

fill –translate –backend –

The translator runs on the filled-in Chinese sentence, so the chain is honest end-to-end: the character BERT predicted is literally part of what gets translated.
