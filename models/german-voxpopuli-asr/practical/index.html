Where was each German word said?

A seq2seq model gives you text but not timing. CTC pins every character to a ~20 ms frame, so the frame each word starts and ends at is a real byproduct of decoding — no extra model. Below, each transcribed German word carries its start–end time. Click a word to hear just that span.

What's happening

The worker records the frame index at which each German character was emitted. Multiply by the frame duration (audio length ÷ frame count) and you get wall-clock timings, grouped into words at the word-boundary symbol. This is genuine forced alignment straight from the acoustic model.
