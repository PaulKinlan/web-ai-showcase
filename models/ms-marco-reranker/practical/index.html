Practical: why a reranker beats embeddings alone

Embedding search is fast but blunt — it compares two vectors made independently, so it can rank a passage high just for sharing a topic. A cross-encoder reads the query and passage together and is far more precise, but too slow to score a whole corpus. The production answer is both: embeddings shortlist, the reranker reorders. Below, watch the same results ranked by embedding cosine and then by the reranker — the column that puts the real answer first is the reranker.
