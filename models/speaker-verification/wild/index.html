Impersonation & voice look-alikes

Voice biometrics aren't magic. Rank a whole line-up against one reference voice and watch for false matches — different speakers the model scores above the same-speaker threshold. Those are exactly where a naïve "voice unlock" would let the wrong person in. Green = truly the same speaker; a red flag = the model is fooled.

    All embeddings are computed on your device. "Truth" labels come from how these public/sample clips are grouped by speaker — a red flag means two different speakers scored above your threshold, i.e. a spoof a real system must defend against with liveness and calibration.

    Why look-alikes matter

    Two people can have genuinely similar voices, and short/noisy clips push different speakers closer together in embedding space. Slide the threshold up and the false matches disappear — but you also start rejecting genuine same-speaker pairs. That tension is the whole story of voice biometrics, and it's why this showcase frames the model as a similarity signal, never an identity decision.
