Voice-unlock, as a signal

Enrol a reference voice (average a few clips), then a probe comes in and the page asks "close enough?" against a tunable threshold. This is the shape of on-device voice re-auth — but it stays a signal. A real unlock needs liveness / anti-spoofing and calibration this demo deliberately omits.

    The enrolled embedding is the average of the reference clips' vectors, kept in memory only. Nothing is uploaded. Adjust the threshold and re-check — the decision recomputes from cached embeddings, no re-inference.

    Why "signal, not verdict"

    Cosine similarity is continuous, and the accept/reject line is a policy choice with real trade-offs: raise the threshold and you reject more impostors but also more genuine users; lower it and the reverse. A shipped system tunes this on its own population, adds liveness to stop a recording being replayed, and audits for bias. This demo shows the raw signal so you can feel those trade-offs by dragging the slider.
