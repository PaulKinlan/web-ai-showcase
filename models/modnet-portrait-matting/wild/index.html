Wild: the hair & fur edge test

Any model can cut out a solid torso. The interesting question is what happens at the fringe — single strands of hair, fur, soft focus. This page composites the subject onto a punchy magenta so every semi-transparent pixel shows, isolates the soft-edge band, and scores how much fine detail the matte actually holds. MODNet is trained on people, so try a portrait first, then throw fur at it.

The matte-detail score is the share of the subject's outline made of partial-alpha (soft) pixels — higher means more hair/fur survived rather than being cut to a hard line. It's a rough proxy, computed on the real matte, not a model confidence.
