Regional slang, sarcasm & emoji

Textbook Spanish is easy. Real Spanish is regional slang, diminutives, irony and emoji — and it differs across Mexico, Argentina, Spain and beyond. Pick a challenge below to see how a Spanish-native model handles it, with the word-level attribution so you can watch what it latched onto. Sarcasm is the honest weak spot: the surface words say one thing, the meaning is the opposite.

Pick a challenge, or type your own

What to look for

  • Slang & diminutives: a Spanish-pretrained model usually reads "chido", "guay", "-ito/-ita" as the signal they carry — a translated English model often misses them.
  • Sarcasm: hardest of all. "Genial, otra vez roto" is positive on the surface, negative in meaning — expect the model to be fooled sometimes. That's honest, not a bug you can hide.
  • Emoji-only: trained on tweets, so 😍 and 🤬 alone still get a verdict.
