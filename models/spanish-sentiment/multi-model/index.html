Multi-model · specialist vs generalist

Spanish-native vs multilingual, head to head

Two models, one input. On the left, RoBERTuito — pre-trained only on Spanish. On the right, Twitter XLM-R — one embedding space across ~8 languages. Type Spanish and both score it at once. On clear text they agree; on idiomatic Spanish — slang, diminutives, regional forms — watch where the specialist and the generalist part. Both run locally in Web Workers; nothing is uploaded.

Reading the comparison

  • Agreement is the common case and reassuring — on plain Spanish both models usually land on the same class.
  • Disagreement is the interesting case: idiomatic or regional Spanish is where a Spanish-pretrained specialist can read a register the generalist flattens (or vice versa on code-mixed text).
  • Neither is "right" by fiat — this is a tool for seeing the trade-off between a language specialist and a one-model-fits-many-languages generalist.
