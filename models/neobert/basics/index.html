Two sentences, one number

Everything an encoder does starts here: embed two pieces of text into 768-d vectors and take the cosine of the angle between them. 1.00 means "same direction / same meaning", lower means further apart. Type two sentences — or pick a pair — and see NeoBERT score how close they are.

Try it

backend –latency –dims –

NeoBERT is a base encoder, not a contrastively fine-tuned sentence model, so its cosines sit in a high, narrow band — a paraphrase and an unrelated pair are both fairly high, but the paraphrase is higher. The ranking is what carries the signal.
