Basics: classification without classes

A normal classifier answers one fixed question. CLAP answers a question you write at runtime. Two small experiments to feel what that means — and where it bends.

1 · Make up the classes, then classify

Pick a sound. The labels below aren't from any dataset — edit them, delete them, invent your own ("a robot having a bad day" is allowed). CLAP scores whatever you give it.

What you just learned

  • No training loop. Adding a class costs one sentence, not a dataset.
  • Scores are relative. The softmax is over your label set — remove the winner and the runner-up inherits its share.
  • Phrasing = prompt engineering. Specific, sound-like descriptions usually beat single words. That's the trade for skipping training.
