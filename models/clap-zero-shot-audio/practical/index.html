Practical: a search engine for sounds

The pattern behind every "find me that clip" feature: embed each audio file once, cache the vectors, then answer text queries with a cosine ranking — no server, no index service, no re-listening. Below, a small library gets embedded in your browser; search it and auto-tag it in plain language. Add your own clips to make it real.

Auto-tag with your vocabulary

The same embeddings, used the other way round: score every clip against a tag vocabulary you control. Change the tags and re-tag — no model re-run on the audio, so it's instant.

Top tag per clip lands in the library table above; ties and low confidence are shown honestly.

Why this is a big deal for products

  • Podcast & media tooling — "find where the audience laughs", across hours, client-side.
  • Sound-effect libraries — stop tagging by hand; search by what it sounds like, not by filename.
  • User-generated content — triage uploads ("music" vs "speech" vs "noise") before anything touches a server.
  • Field recording & research — wildlife or urban-noise surveys searchable the day they're recorded, offline.
