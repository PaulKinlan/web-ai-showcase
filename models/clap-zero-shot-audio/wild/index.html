Wild: a detector for anything you can say

Sound detectors used to be products: a company trained "glass break" or "baby cry" into a device and that's all it would ever know. Here, the detector is a text box. Type a phrase, go live, and your browser watches the mic for it — then pit two phrases against each other in a tug-of-war. Nothing is uploaded; there is nothing to upload to.

2 · Tug-of-war: two phrases fight over the mic

Two descriptions, one microphone. Every couple of seconds CLAP decides which phrase fits the current sound better, and the rope moves. Try "music" vs "talking" and switch what you're doing — or make it weird: "happy sounds" vs "sad sounds".

Why this couldn't exist before

A phrase-programmable sound detector needed three impossible things at once: a model that maps language and audio into one space (contrastive pretraining, 2022+), a runtime that executes it on any laptop without installs (WASM/WebGPU in the browser), and a privacy story that lets a microphone run all day (nothing leaves the device). All three are on this page. The gap between "an idea for a detector" and "a working detector" is now the time it takes to type the phrase.
