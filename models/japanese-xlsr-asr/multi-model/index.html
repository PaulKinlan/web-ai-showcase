Multi-model: Japanese speech → English meaning

One model rarely does the whole job. Here XLSR-53 turns Japanese speech into Japanese text, then OPUS-MT (MarianMT) turns that Japanese text into English. Speech in a language you may not read becomes something you understand — two models chained in your browser, nothing uploaded.

What's happening

Both models run in their own Web Workers, so neither the CTC forward pass nor the autoregressive translation decoder blocks the page. The only thing crossing between them is a short Japanese string. This is the shape of real multilingual understanding on the edge: a speech model plus a translation model, composed client-side, working offline once both are cached.
