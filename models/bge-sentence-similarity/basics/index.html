Basics: do these two sentences mean the same thing?

The atom of everything BGE does. Give it two sentences; it turns each into a 384-number vector and reports the cosine similarity — 1.00 means "same direction in meaning space", near 0 means "unrelated". Below that, a demo of why this beats keyword matching: high word overlap can still mean opposite things, and no shared words can still mean the same thing.

2 · Meaning beats keywords

A query, and two candidates: one shares lots of words with the query but means something different; the other shares no words but means the same. Keyword search picks the wrong one — BGE picks the right one.

Cosine similarity ranges from -1 to 1, but for BGE most real sentence pairs land between about 0.3 (unrelated) and 0.95 (near-paraphrase) — its scores sit higher and more separated than MiniLM's, which is part of why it retrieves better. Note "masterpiece" vs "terrible" still scores fairly high: they share a topic (a film) even though the opinions flip. Embeddings capture aboutness, not agreement.
