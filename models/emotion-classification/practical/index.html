Practical: emotion-based message routing

A star rating tells you whether someone is happy; the emotion tells you what to do. Paste a queue of messages (one per line) and each is scored and routed by its single dominant emotion — anger/disgust → escalate, fear/sadness → empathy, joy/surprise → celebrate, neutral → standard. One batched pass, on-device.

Routing on emotion catches what a rating can't: a calm 3-star that's actually frightened about data loss (respond with care) versus one that's angry (escalate now). The whole queue is scored in a single forward pass, so it scales to hundreds of messages without one server call.
