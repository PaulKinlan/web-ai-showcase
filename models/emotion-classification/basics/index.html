Basics: one feeling wins, live

The simplest thing the model does: read a piece of text and pick the single strongest of the seven Ekman emotions, with a confidence. Type in the box and the verdict updates a moment after you pause. Because it's a softmax, the seven scores compete — nudge the wording and watch the winner change.

Because it was trained on short, emotionally-charged text, the model is decisive on clear feelings and leans on neutral for flat, factual sentences. The confidence — and the shape of the wheel — tells you how close the runners-up were.
