Emotion vs sentiment: nuance against one axis

Binary sentiment squashes every feeling onto a single positive↔negative line. This 7-emotion model keeps the shape. Run the same text through both — DistilBERT SST-2 for POSITIVE/NEGATIVE and Emotion DistilRoBERTa for the winning Ekman emotion — and watch where they agree and where sentiment throws information away. Two models, both local, nothing uploaded.

Why chain them

Sentiment is a great filter — cheap, decisive, easy to threshold. Emotion is the explanation. Chained, you get both: sort by sentiment, then act on emotion. "Negative" is a bucket; "fear about data loss" is a next step. Notice the mixed cases — "terrified but I trust you" reads as one blur to sentiment, but the emotion wheel keeps the fear and the trust visible.
