Irony + sentiment: recovering the true meaning

Sentiment reads the words; irony tells you whether to believe them. Chain the two: score the surface sentiment with DistilBERT SST-2, check for sarcasm with Twitter-RoBERTa irony, and when the text is ironic, flip the polarity to recover what the writer actually meant. Both models run locally; nothing leaves the tab.

Each model initialises on its own below (cached copies auto-start, no button).

Why this is the interesting part

Neither model can do this alone. Sentiment alone calls sarcastic praise "positive"; irony alone tells you something is off but not which way. The rule — if ironic and confident, invert the surface polarity — is a tiny piece of glue that turns two 100-MB classifiers into a sarcasm-aware sentiment reader. It's a toy rule (real systems would weight by confidence and defer to a human near the boundary), but it shows the compositional move: small local models, chained, beat any one of them.
