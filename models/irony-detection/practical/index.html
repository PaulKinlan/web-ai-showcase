Practical: a sarcasm pre-check for sentiment

Sentiment models read words. Sarcasm inverts them — "I just love being ignored for a week" is positive vocabulary, negative meaning. Before you trust a sentiment score, run a cheap sarcasm pre-check: paste a queue of reviews (one per line) and each is flagged ironic (handle with care) or clear (safe to score literally). The flagged rows are the ones a naive sentiment model would get backwards. In a real pipeline you'd route them to a human, downweight them, or hand them to the irony + sentiment flow that flips the polarity automatically. The point isn't perfect sarcasm detection — it's catching the obvious inversions cheaply before they poison an aggregate score.
