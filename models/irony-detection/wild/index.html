Wild: the hard cases — deadpan and understatement

Stock sarcasm ("oh great") is easy. The interesting failures are deadpan (sarcasm with a straight face), understatement ("well, that could have gone better" after a disaster), and lines that are only ironic if you know the situation. Score the gallery below — the ones near the 50% line are where the model, like a human without tone of voice, genuinely can't tell.

A confident wrong answer and an honest 50/50 are different failure modes. The model was trained on tweets, where sarcasm is often signposted; strip the signposts (deadpan) and it leans literal. That's the ceiling of text-only sarcasm detection — the missing signal is tone, and no amount of vocabulary recovers it.
