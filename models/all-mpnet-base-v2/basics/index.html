Basics: one number for "same meaning?"

The simplest thing all-mpnet-base-v2 does: turn two sentences into 768-number vectors and measure the cosine between them. A faithful paraphrase scores high even with zero shared words; unrelated text scores low even when words overlap. Below that, the same trick answers a different question: which sentence doesn't belong?

Cosine runs from −1 to 1; for all-mpnet-base-v2, paraphrases typically land around 0.55–0.9 and unrelated pairs below 0.25. There's no hard threshold — it's a smooth similarity, and you pick the cut-off that fits your task. Note the "same word, different meaning" chip: keyword search would call those a match; the embedding doesn't.

Odd one out

Embed every line, average all the vectors, and the line furthest from the average is the odd one out. Edit the list — the model finds the intruder by meaning, not by vocabulary.
