Wild: games you can only play in embedding space

Before models ran in browsers you could never ship this: a guessing game where the referee is a neural network's notion of meaning, running privately on the player's device. Below it, the old word2vec party trick — king − man + woman — attempted on whole sentences, with an honest look at where it works and where it falls apart.

Semantic Hot & Cold

A secret phrase is hidden in this page's memory (never shown, never sent anywhere — there is no server). Type guesses; the model embeds each one and reports pure cosine warmth. Chase the meaning, not the words.

round – guesses 0 best warmth –

Warmth is the raw cosine between your guess and the secret phrase — nothing is scripted. ≥ 0.75 counts as found. Every round is a real inference; watch the latency in the history rows.

Sentence algebra: A − B + C

Compute vector(A) − vector(B) + vector(C), re-normalize, and rank the candidate lines by cosine to the result. With word2vec this gave king − man + woman ≈ queen; sentence encoders aren't trained for arithmetic, so treat this as a probe — the default works, but push it and you'll find the edges (that's the point).
