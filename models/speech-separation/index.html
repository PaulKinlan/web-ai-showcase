Speech separation (cocktail party)

The built speech enhancement demo cleans noise off one voice. This one solves the harder cocktail-party problem: given a recording of two people talking at once, it pulls them apart into two clean single-speaker tracks. It's ConvTasNet, a lightweight time-domain separator. Below, two speakers are mixed in your browser, then separated — entirely on your device.

Why raw ONNX Runtime Web?

transformers.js has no speech-separation task, so we run ConvTasNet's ONNX graph directly with onnxruntime-web. Unlike spectrogram methods, ConvTasNet works in the time domain: a learned encoder turns the waveform into features, a mask network splits them into two streams, and a decoder turns each back into audio. It's small and fast, and every sample stays on your device.
