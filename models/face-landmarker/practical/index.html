Practical: an attention / drowsiness monitor

The mesh isn't the point — the blendshapes are. This reads three signals the model already gives you — how closed the eyes are (eyeBlink), how open the jaw is (jawOpen), and which way the head is turned — and turns them into a live attention state: attentive, eyes closing, yawning, or looking away. The kind of thing a driver-monitoring or focus tool does, entirely on-device.

Where this goes

Because it runs on-device, none of the video ever leaves the machine — which is exactly what you want for driver drowsiness alerts, reading-attention tools for study apps, video-call "are you still there?" nudges, or accessibility triggers. The thresholds here are deliberately simple; a shipping product would smooth them over time and calibrate per person.
