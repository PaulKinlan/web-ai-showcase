Wild: code-switched text

Real multilingual writing rarely stays in one language — people switch mid-sentence (a WhatsApp message that starts in Spanish and ends in English, a tweet that drops a Chinese brand name into a French sentence). A per-language pipeline would have to pick one language and get the rest wrong. Because this model shares one 104-language vocabulary, it just keeps tagging through the switch. Pick a mixed sample and watch it hold up.

Code-switching is where single-language NER breaks and this shared-vocabulary model shines — but it's also where confidences dip, so hover the highlights. The switch point itself sometimes confuses the tagger (a name right at the boundary), which is exactly the kind of edge this demo is here to expose.
