How close did the AI get?

Here's a game only a browser can make instant: take a colour photo, throw its colour away, ask DDColor to guess it back, then reveal the real colours and score the match. You'll see exactly where the model is confident (sky, foliage, skin) and where it's just guessing (clothes, painted objects).

Only gallery images have a "true colour" to score against. Your own uploads are colourised too, but there's no ground truth to compare — the score needs the original colours.

What the score means

The match score compares the model's predicted chroma to the photo's real chroma, pixel by pixel (in CIE-Lab, so it tracks perceived colour). 100 would be a perfect colour match; in practice a good guess lands in the 70s–90s because the model reconstructs the type of scene rather than the exact hues. It is a fun proxy, not a scientific metric — a plausible-but-different colourisation can score lower than a dull one and still look better.
