How big a hole can it fill?

LaMa was built for large masks — that's its whole thesis. Grow the masked rectangle step by step and watch where the fill stays coherent versus where the model runs out of surrounding context and starts inventing smooth, plausible-but-empty texture.

Run it

Set the hole size, Inpaint, and log the result. Larger holes → more of the image is invented rather than reconstructed from context.

Where it breaks

Small holes are near-perfect — there's plenty of surrounding context to continue. As the hole grows past roughly half the image, LaMa has less real structure to anchor on, so the centre of large fills goes soft and generic. That's the honest limit of inpainting: it interpolates and extrapolates surrounding structure; it can't reconstruct a subject that was entirely inside the mask.
