Wild: translation telephone

Take a sentence and pass it through a chain of languages — English to French to Japanese to Arabic and back to English — one hop at a time. Each translation is faithful, but tiny choices compound, and by the end the meaning has often wandered somewhere delightful. Watch it drift.

Comma-separated M2M100 codes. Start is English; the chain ends wherever you like (end with en to come home).

Idioms and puns drift fastest — they lean on meanings that don't survive a literal hop. Plain, concrete sentences are far more robust. That contrast is a real window into what machine translation preserves and what it quietly reinterprets.
