Pull tables and formulas straight out of a page

The parts of a document that break flat OCR are exactly where DocTags shine. Point SmolDocling at a page with a table and it emits <otsl> cells you can drop into a spreadsheet; point it at a printed equation and it emits <formula> as LaTeX. All from a picture, all on-device.

What's happening

The same single DocTags pass produces tables and formulas alongside the prose; we just filter for the <otsl> and <formula> elements. OTSL (Optimised Table Structure Language) encodes each cell and row break, so the model recovers the grid — not just the cell text — which is why the result drops cleanly into a real table. Formulas come back as LaTeX, ready to re-typeset or search.
