Grapheme-to-phoneme (spelling → sound)

The built text models read and rewrite words. This one predicts how a word is pronounced — turning spelling into IPA phonemes, the front-end step every text-to-speech system needs. It's CharsiuG2P, a multilingual byT5 that works on raw bytes, so it phonemizes any script — Latin, Cyrillic, kana, Greek. Type a word, pick a language, and see its sounds. Runs entirely on your device.

Why byte-level?

CharsiuG2P is a byT5 — it has no learned word vocabulary. Text is just UTF-8 bytes (each byte → id = byte + 3), so a Cyrillic, Japanese, or Greek word is as encodable as an English one, and the model can phonemize all of them. It's a small (tiny) model decoding one word at a time, so the odd rare word may get a trailing artifact — the output is the model's real prediction, not a dictionary lookup. Everything runs locally.
