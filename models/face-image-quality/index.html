Face image-quality assessment

Before a system tries to recognise a face, it asks a cheaper question: is this capture even good enough? A blurry, dark, or off-angle face wastes compute and produces unreliable matches. Face image-quality assessment scores exactly that — a single number for how usable a face crop is. eDifFIQA(T) is a tiny (~7 MB) model distilled from a diffusion-based quality teacher. Rank real faces below, then blur or darken one and watch the score fall. Runs entirely on your device; nothing leaves the tab.

Why raw ONNX Runtime Web?

Transformers.js has no face-IQA model class or task, so we run eDifFIQA's ONNX graph directly with onnxruntime-web: crop the face to 112×112, pack it as [1,3,112,112] float32 in 0–1, and read the scalar quality output. It doesn't identify anyone — it only judges the capture — and it all happens on your device.
