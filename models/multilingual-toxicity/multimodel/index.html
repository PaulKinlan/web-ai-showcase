Multi-model: a safety gate before analysis

Two multilingual models, one pipeline, zero servers. The multilingual toxicity model acts as a gate: it scores every comment first, and only text that comes back clean is passed through to the multilingual sentiment model for analysis. Toxic text is held for review instead — you don't want an abusive line silently feeding your analytics. Type a mix of languages and watch each comment take one of the two paths.

1 · Toxicity gate (mBERT · 15 languages)

2 · Sentiment (XLM-R · multilingual)

total – passed to sentiment – held (toxic) – time –

This is a common production shape — a cheap safety filter in front of a more expensive model or a human queue — but done entirely locally, so no comment leaves the tab and there's no per-call bill. Swap the downstream model for translation, summarisation, or an LLM and the gate pattern is the same.
