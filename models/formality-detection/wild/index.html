Wild: find the register crossover

Register isn't binary in real writing — it's a slope. Write the same message several ways, from a text to a mate to a line in a legal letter, and score them all at once. The model sorts them along the meter and marks the crossover — the exact point where it stops calling the wording informal and starts calling it formal. That boundary is where the interesting, borderline cases live.

backend – variants – latency –

Variants are sorted by the model's formal-probability and shown with their score. The crossover line marks where the verdict flips from informal to formal — often between two rewrites that feel almost identical, which is exactly what makes register a hard, gradient signal rather than a switch.
