What you're seeing

At each step the model produces a probability distribution over the next token. Low temperature sharpens it — the most likely token almost always wins, so the output is repeatable and safe. High temperature flattens it — unlikely tokens get a real chance, so you get novelty (and, past a point, nonsense). There's no "correct" setting: extraction and code want cold, brainstorming and fiction want warmth. Running both at once makes the trade-off tangible instead of theoretical.
