SegFormer × Depth Anything: a scene map, shaded by distance

Two views of one scene. SegFormer answers "what is each pixel?" (sky, tree, road…); Depth Anything answers "how far is each pixel?". Multiply them and you get a map coloured by class and shaded by depth — near things bright, far things dark — a compact picture of the scene's layout, entirely on your device.

Each model runs once, in its own Web Worker, in parallel. The combined map is a pure canvas composite — class colour × a brightness taken from the depth map. Nothing is uploaded.

How the two connect

The two models are independent — neither knows the other exists — but their outputs line up pixel for pixel on the same image, so they compose for free. Semantics answer what; depth answers where in space. Together they're the raw material for depth-aware relighting, ground-plane detection, or a quick "is the sky actually far away?" sanity check on a scene.
