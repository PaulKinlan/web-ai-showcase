Practical: privacy redaction

A concrete thing you could ship: detect a class of object — people, screens, licence plates' parent objects — and blur or black-box every instance before the image leaves the browser. The detection and the redaction both happen on-device; the original pixels never travel.

Blur copies a heavily blurred version of just the detected regions over the original; black box paints solid. Either way the underlying pixels in those regions are gone from the exported image.
