DETR × SlimSAM: detect, then segment

Compose two models into one flow. DETR answers "what's here and roughly where" with labelled boxes; then you tap the object you care about and SlimSAM answers "which exact pixels" with a precise mask. Detection gives the semantics, segmentation gives the silhouette — both entirely on your device.

SlimSAM encodes the image once (in parallel with detection); tapping a box just runs the light mask decoder at that object's centre. All inference is in Web Workers — one per model.

How the two connect

DETR's output is a list of boxes with class labels. Each box's centre is a natural point prompt for SAM: "segment whatever is here." That is the entire bridge — no shared weights, no retraining, just one model's output becoming another's input. The same pattern generalises: detect → segment → classify the crop, or detect → segment → cut out and recompose.
