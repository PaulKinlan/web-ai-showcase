Age-aware default — a signal, never a verdict

The wrong way to use an age model is a hard gate on its number. The right way: treat the estimate as a signal that pre-selects a friendlier default, then always let the person see and change it. Here the on-device estimate nudges a "content mode" default — and the confirm step is what actually decides. Nothing is uploaded; only the chosen mode would ever be kept.

🔒 Frames are read locally and never uploaded or stored.

Why this pattern

  • Signal, not verdict. The model only pre-selects; the confirm click is the real decision, so a wrong estimate is a one-tap fix, not a lockout.
  • Confidence-gated. An ambiguous read (high entropy / low margin) pre-selects nothing and asks the person — the model never over-reaches.
  • Private by design. The frame is classified locally and discarded; only the confirmed mode would be persisted, never a face or an age.
  • Not legal verification. Real age-assurance for restricted goods has regulatory requirements this cannot meet — this is UX personalisation, nothing more.
