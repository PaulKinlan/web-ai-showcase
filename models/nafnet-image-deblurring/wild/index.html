Why it breaks

Blur is a lossy operation: it averages neighbouring pixels, and past a point that average is irreversible — the fine detail simply isn't in the pixels anymore. NAFNet recovers what it can from context and its training, so "recovery" (how much of the lost sharpness it puts back) falls as blur grows. That's honest model behaviour: restoration is a rescue, not a time machine.
