Two schools of stylization, one photo

Two very different ways to restyle an image, run on the same photo: a classic fast-neural-style painting network (Johnson 2016, one model per artwork) and AnimeGANv2 (a GAN that repaints photos as anime). Why compare them

Both are "stylization", but they're different techniques: fast-neural-style is a per-artwork feed-forward network trained with perceptual loss to match one painting's texture; AnimeGANv2 is a GAN trained on a whole animator's films. Seeing them on the same photo shows how differently two model families interpret the word "style" — and both run entirely in your browser.
