Wild: the Mad-Libs machine

Give ALBERT a template riddled with [MASK] blanks and it fills them one at a time, each choice conditioned on the words it already committed to — so the sentence grows into something coherent-ish and often funny. Set the mischief dial to sample from further down the ranking for weirder results, and reroll for a new take. All on device.

backend – latency – blanks –

Each blank is filled left to right: ALBERT predicts the first [MASK], we drop the word in, then it predicts the next one with that word already there. The mischief dial picks randomly from the top-k instead of always taking #1, so higher = stranger. That's autoregressive decoding faked out of a bidirectional model — a genuinely wild use of fill-mask.
