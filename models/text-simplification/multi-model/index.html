Simplify, then speak it aloud

The complete accessibility pipeline from two on-device models. First the FLAN-T5 simplifier rewrites dense text into plain language; then MMS-TTS reads the plain version aloud. Simpler words and spoken — the two things that most help a struggling reader — produced privately, on your device, with nothing sent to a server.

Why compose them?

Reading aloud helps, but reading aloud dense text just makes a listener sit through the same impenetrable sentence more slowly. Simplify first, and the spoken output is genuinely easier to follow — shorter words, simpler clauses. It's a small, concrete example of chaining on-device models into an assistive tool that never phones home.
